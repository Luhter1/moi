  So, a couple of years ago I had a really bad experience at a hospital. I'd been having this pain in my stomach for about two days, and at some point it became excruciating, so my friend    
  called an ambulance and I was rushed to hospital.                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                               
  After a quick check, they told me to wait. And I waited for six hours — even though the place wasn't even that busy. When the doctor finally saw me, he barely looked at me. He was typing on
   his computer the whole time. I tried to explain how I was feeling, but he just said it was probably a stomach bug or something like that, and told me to take painkillers. That was it —    
  maybe three minutes, tops.                                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                                               
  I was too shocked to argue, so I just left. But the next morning the pain was worse, so I went to a different hospital. Turns out it was appendicitis and I needed an emergency operation. I 
  was put on a drip, given an anaesthetic and they removed my appendix that same evening. The doctor there said I was lucky I came in when I did.                                              
                                                                                                                                                                                               
  Looking back, I think the first doctor was probably just tired — it was a weekend night, so he must've been exhausted. But that's not an excuse. This could have been avoided if there were  
  more doctors on duty and shorter waiting times. I filed a complaint later and got a letter saying there had been a mix-up with my assessment. In spite of the apology, the whole thing was   
  really emotionally draining, and it made me lose a bit of faith in the system.
